Year 800

Foundation & Sheepfold Economy

Goal: Build your Sheepfolds, establish food and wood income, and start your feast engine. Goat's sheep provide year-round food bonuses — lean into this from the start.

1
Build Scout Camp
Scout all adjacent tiles. Goat starts with one sheep and the ability to build up to two Sheepfolds — find good tiles for them immediately.
50 Wood
2
Build Sheepfold
Your core food building. Sheepfolds provide a year-round food bonus for sheep — unlike farms and hunters, they don't suffer winter penalties. Build both Sheepfolds as early as possible.
60 Wood
3
Expand Out
Prioritize tiles with Jötnar and Dwarven camps — Goat can use sheep to gain their support for extra bonuses and abilities. Also look for lore stones.
20 Food
🐐

Sheep are your economy: Goat's sheep provide year-round food bonuses that don't suffer winter penalties. At 200 Fame, you gain 2 additional sheep and your Sheepfold limit increases to 3. Prioritize getting to 200 Fame quickly.

4
Build Woodcutter's Lodge
Assign two villagers. Wood is needed for more buildings throughout 801.
50 Wood
5
First Lore: Simple Living
Reduces food consumption, which is critical for sustaining expansion in the early game. Goat's food surplus from sheep makes this even more powerful.
Lore
6
Build Docks or Trading Post
Steady Krown income funds Harvald and building upgrades. Goat's economy is flexible — adapt to your map.
80 Wood
7
Summon Harvald
Harvald is your warchief — high defense and health substitute his lower attack. He can capture sheep in neutral or enemy zones, expanding your flock. Get him out as soon as you have the resources.
Krowns + Iron
🐐

Feast bonus: Goat's feasts offer a 20% increase in production. Combined with the Eldhrumnir relic (which provides an additional 20% production buff while feasting), feasts become one of the most powerful economic tools in the game.

Year 801

Economy & Reach 200 Fame

Goal: Hit 200 Fame to unlock 2 additional sheep and increase your Sheepfold limit to 3. Your sheep's food production also increases by 10% — a significant economic boost.

1
Build third Sheepfold (after 200 Fame)
At 200 Fame, your Sheepfold limit increases to 3. Build the third Sheepfold immediately and assign your new sheep to it.
60 Wood
2
Feast in November
Goat's feasts give +20% production. Coordinate with your team — if you feast, everyone benefits. The Eldhrumnir relic reduces feast costs and adds another +20% production buff.
Food varies
3
Use Harvald to capture sheep
Harvald can capture sheep in neutral or enemy zones. Use him to expand your flock beyond the normal limit. More sheep means more food production.
4
Build Forge & upgrade tools
Upgrade food production and Sailors. Goat's economy scales well with tool upgrades and feast bonuses.
60 Wood Iron + Krowns
5
Build Training Camp for defense
Goat's military is weaker than other clans, but you need a presence. Build a Training Camp and keep 5–7 units in your warband to deter early rushes.
50 Wood
🐐

Lore priority for 801:

1st Simple Living — reduces food consumption, sustaining expansion
2nd Colonization — –30% food cost to colonize new territories
3rd Lay of the Land — increases production bonus from Forests, Swamps, and Fish tiles
4th Weaponsmith — +20% attack for all military units
Year 802

Economy Scaling & Feast Engine

Goal: Scale your feast engine with the Eldhrumnir relic, build out your military presence, and push toward 500 Fame for a free annual feast that also heals your units.

1
Forge Eldhrumnir relic
Provides a 20% production buff while feasting, in addition to reducing feast costs. Combined with Goat's base +20% feast bonus, this gives +40% production during feasts — one of the strongest economic buffs in the game.
Iron + Krowns
2
Feast frequently
With Eldhrumnir reducing feast costs, you can feast more often. Coordinate with your team — Goat's feast buffs apply to all allied units in your territory.
Food varies
3
Build Altar of Kings
Unlocks additional lore paths. Goat's lore tree has powerful economic bonuses — prioritize Bartering and Handiwork for maximum economy scaling.
Wood + Stone
4
Use Jötnar and Dwarven support
Goat can use sheep to gain the support of Jötnar and Dwarven camps for extra bonuses and abilities. Prioritize finding these camps and building relations with them.
⚔

500 Fame: Free annual feast: At 500 Fame, Goat receives a free feast each year that also heals your units. This is a massive sustained bonus — prioritize reaching 500 Fame as quickly as possible.

Year 803

Sustained Economy & Map Control

Goal: Sustain your feast engine, keep your sheep producing, and support your team with production buffs. Goat is a strong support-economy clan in the late game.

1
Keep feasting regularly
With the free annual feast at 500 Fame and Eldhrumnir reducing costs, you should be feasting every 2–3 months. The +40% production buff during feasts is your team's most powerful economic tool.
2
Sustain military presence
Keep 8–10 military units active. Goat's military isn't the strongest, but a visible presence deters raids and buys time for your team to scale.
3
Coordinate with team to close out
Goat's feast buffs and economic support are most valuable when your team has strong military carries. Keep feasting and let your team do the fighting.
Year 804

Endgame Support

Goal: Keep feasting and supporting your team. Goat wins through Trade or Lore Victory, or by enabling your team's military carries to close out.

1
Keep the feast engine running
Free annual feast + Eldhrumnir = constant +40% production buffs for your team. This is your most valuable contribution in 804.
2
Push for Trade or Lore Victory
Goat's economy makes Trade and Lore Victories very achievable. Build a Lighthouse for Trade Victory or maximize your lore buildings for Lore Victory.
