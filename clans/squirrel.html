Year 800

Foundation & First Cats

Goal: Get Brundr & Kaelinn on the map immediately, start clearing neutral tiles for Hunting Trophies, and establish food + wood income before winter hits.

1
Build Scout Camp
Your very first build. Scout out all tiles connected to your Town Hall. Assign Scouts to Town Hall to gather food.
50 Wood
2
Expand Out
You can expand while scouting if you see a Lore Stone or any Ruins(Tombs or Shipwrecks). Otherwise wait until you see Lore or Food.
20 Food
3
Build House and/or Archery Range
If you started with a ship wreck build an Archery Range. Otherwise build a House so your population can continue to expand.
50 Wood
🐾

Recruiting Cats:Your Brundr & Kaelinn costs 100 krowns and food TOTAL. Begin building the Archery Range around 80 krowns. Only spend food when you need to.

4
Build Woodcutter's Lodge
Delay this until you have around 80 Wood remaining so you can squeeze extra food-gathering time first. Assign two villagers. Wood is needed for buildings throughout 801.
50 Wood
5
First Lore and Hunter's Lodge
Build a Hunter's Lodge and take Sharp Axes. You'll want Spoils of Plenty so you don't have to rely on native food tiles.
50 Wood
6
Build Docks and Wood Cutters
You will want 100 krowns to recruit Mielikki. You will also need a lot of wood to build more camps and houses
80 Wood
7
Summon Mielikki (if gold allows)
If you can reach 100 gold before winter — via a nearby treasure chest, fox burrow, or dock — get Mielikki out in 800. She costs no iron, unlike most warchiefs.
100 Gold
🐾

Clearing priority: Middle tile first, then right, then left. Use Brundr & Kaelinn for all clearing — keep your villagers on food and wood. Every animal the cats kill awards Hunting Trophies that unlock your Path of the Hunter upgrades.

⚠

Upgrade 1 house you want the population to convert 801-802.

Year 801

Economy & Reach 200 Fame

Goal: Hit 200 Fame as fast as possible to unlock the feast discount. Establish your krown economy and build out a sustainable mid-game.

1
Build Longship Dock(s)
If you have 2+ coastal tiles, build a second docks for steady gold income. Otherwise build a Trading Post. Gold funds Trackers and Mielikki if you haven't summoned her yet.
80 Wood
2
Take your 3rd Lore & more
Now go down the middle tree. The most important Lores are "Archery Mastery" and "Nimble
Lore
3
Colonize aggressively & Clear
Expand toward wolf dens and deer tiles. While you're expanding clear your teammates, you should be doing this as soon as you have your cats.
Food per tile Trophies passive
5
Feast in November (unless a teammate covers it)
Do the November feast to accelerate your Fame gain. After hitting 200 Fame, every animal kill reduces the cost of your next feast — meaning future feasts become significantly cheaper the more you hunt.
Food varies
6
Build Healer's Hut
Mielikki is weaker than most warchiefs — keep her alive with a healer nearby. Also needed to keep Brundr & Kaelinn healthy during aggressive clearing pushes into harder camps.
60 Wood
7
Hire first Tracker
Trackers are your core ranged combat unit, produced at the Archery Range. They get stronger with every wildlife kill.
~300 Gold for 7
🦌

Lore priority summary for 801:

1st Weaponsmith — +20% attack for all military units
2nd Archery Mastery — Your first Tracker is always free. Reduces Archery Range Archery Range's upgrade cost by 50%.
3rd Feeling Safe Gain +3 Happiness if you have a Warchief and +1 Happiness per upgraded military camp.
4th Nimble Paws Brundr and Kaelinn gain +35% max health. They also frequently leap, dodging all attacks while airbone.
Year 802

Full Tracker Convert & Military Build-Up

Goal: Mass convert Trackers, upgrade the Forge and Archery Range, and prepare to contest or rush enemy positions. This is your power window.

1
Convert up to 7 Trackers
300 gold funds 7 Trackers. This is your primary military force. Their strength scales with Hunting Trophies accumulated, so if you've been clearing consistently, they hit significantly harder than generic units.
300 Gold
2
Build Forge & upgrade tools
Upgrade Woodcutters, Hunters, Sailors, and Trackers. Tool upgrades compound your existing production bonuses. With Spoils of Plenty active, Hunter tool upgrades cost less.
80 Wood Iron + Gold
3
Upgrade Archery Range
Upgrading the Archery Range increases warband slots. Take the Archery Mastery lore if available — it cuts the upgrade cost by 50%. More slots means more Trackers in a single engagement.
Stone + Wood
4
Build second Stone Mine
Only if you're not under rush pressure. Stone fuels your building upgrades. If an enemy is pushing early, hold off and keep villagers on food/military rather than stretching economy.
60 Wood
5
Build second Longship Dock + Altar
A second dock accelerates gold income significantly. The Altar unlocks additional lore paths. At this point your economy should be stable enough to run both.
Wood + Stone
6
Rush Fire Arrows lore (aggressive build)
If you're playing an aggressive rush build: go straight down the middle lore path, skip mining upgrades entirely, and prioritize Fire Arrows. Your Trackers become a much harder-hitting frontline threat.
Lore
⚔

Rush vs. Scale decision point: If the map is an 802 game (enemy rushes expected), prioritize Fire Arrows and push early. If you have space to scale, hold back and keep converting Trackers until you have 14+ in your warband before engaging.

Year 803

Sustained Army & Map Control

Goal: Sustain 14+ Trackers, keep feasting to support allies, and use Mielikki's Lure to farm mythical animals for both trophies and economic bonuses.

1
Sustain 14+ Trackers in warband
Keep military camps upgraded and cycle gold income directly into maintaining this number. Trackers with full trophy upgrades are one of the strongest sustained ranged forces in the game at this point.
2
Upgrade Town Hall & key buildings
Upgrade Town Hall for population growth. Also upgrade at least one Dock, one House, and the Archery Range if not done in 802. The population growth speed lore (+25%) compounds well here.
3
Place Lynx Traps on enemy-adjacent neutral tiles
Traps placed on unclaimed tiles near enemy borders passively generate trophies and can surprise enemies who push through those tiles. A subtle but effective harassment tool mid-game.
Trophy generation
4
Continue feast cycle with teammates
After 200 Fame, each animal kill lowers your next feast cost. Coordinate with your team — if a Stag or Goat player feasts, stockpile food for tile colonization instead. If not, you feast and buff the whole team.
5
Use Kaelinn to clear Valkyries & Draugr Tombs
Kaelinn's mobility makes her excellent at taking down high-value neutral camps. If a camp is too strong for her alone, bring one Tracker for support. These camps drop significant resources and lore.
⚠

Watch for axe throwers: By 803–804, enemy ax throwers become a serious threat — they out-damage your Trackers at range by default. Rush the movement speed upgrade for archers so you can kite and reposition groups in combat rather than standing and trading fire.

Year 804

500 Fame — Oskoreia & Endgame

Goal: Reach 500 Fame to unlock Mielikki's Oskoreia ability. Summon mythical spirit animals for a devastating final push, or use your scaled Tracker army to close out a Dominion or Fame victory.

1
Unlock Oskoreia (500 Fame milestone)
Mielikki's 500 Fame bonus unlocks Oskoreia — she recalls the strongest wave of mythical animals your clan has killed. They return in spirit form for one final rampage. This is your endgame spike ability.
500 Fame
2
Finalize Path of the Hunter upgrades
Spend remaining Hunting Trophies to max out Brundr, Kaelinn, and Tracker upgrades. The final trophy tier unlocks Mythical Lure — attracting the most powerful creatures to your territory for farming.
Hunting Trophies
3
Military surge with 14–18 Trackers
A full warband of upgraded Trackers with Fire Arrows and movement speed is capable of overwhelming most compositions. Combine with Oskoreia's spirit animals for a coordinated two-front assault.
4
Push for victory condition
Lynx wins most naturally via Dominion (territory control) or Fame. Keep feasting, keep hunting, and keep your Trackers in the field. A strong Lynx player by year 804 is difficult to stop.
🌿

Oskoreia timing: Save the ability for a decisive moment — either to break an enemy chokepoint or to defend your base during a siege. The spirit animals are powerful but only last one engagement, so don't waste it on a minor skirmish.
