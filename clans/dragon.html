Year 800

Foundation & Thrall Economy

Goal: Establish your Thrall economy, build your Sacrificial Pyre, and start generating Dragon Essence. Dragon is the most complex clan — balance is everything.

1
Build Scout Camp
Scout all adjacent tiles. You start with a Sacrificial Pyre and 2 Thralls — assign them to food gathering immediately.
50 Wood
2
Expand Out
Expand to food tiles. Dragon's civilians can enter neutral territory without Happiness penalties — use this to gather resources from unclaimed zones.
20 Food
3
Build Dragonkin Altar
This replaces your military camp. Assign villagers here to convert them into Dragonkin — your primary military unit. Each conversion generates Dragon Essence.
50 Wood
🐉

Dragon Essence gauge: Keep your Dragon Essence gauge balanced. Too low and you lose bonuses; too high and you can sacrifice units for powerful effects. The Sacrificial Pyre lets you sacrifice units to gain Dragon Essence — use it strategically, not recklessly.

4
Build Woodcutter's Lodge
Assign two villagers. Wood is needed for more Dragonkin Altars and buildings throughout 801.
50 Wood
5
Buy Thralls from Longship Docks
Build a Longship Dock and buy additional Thralls. Thralls don't require housing and consume only 33% of normal food — they're your economic backbone. Assign them to production buildings to boost output.
80 Wood Krowns per Thrall
6
Summon Surtr
Surtr is your warchief, summoned at the Dragonkin Altar. He's a powerful frontline fighter. Get him out as soon as you have the resources.
Krowns + Iron
🐉

Thrall management: Thralls produce 50% less than villagers but cost almost nothing to maintain. Stack them on food and wood production to free up your villagers for military conversion. Keep buying Thralls from your Dock whenever you have spare Krowns.

⚠

Maintain Krowns: Dragon is the most Krown-hungry clan in the game. Thralls, Dragonkin, and Surtr all cost Krowns. Build your economy before going heavy on military.

Year 801

Dragonkin Army & Reach 200 Fame

Goal: Hit 200 Fame to receive 5 free Thralls and extend your Dragon Essence gauge. Build out your Dragonkin force and start pressuring the map.

1
Build second Dragonkin Altar
More Altars means more Dragonkin in your warband. Dragon's military scales with the number of Dragonkin you can field simultaneously.
50 Wood
2
Convert 5–7 Dragonkin
Dragonkin have a Whirlwind ability — a swirling strike that damages all surrounding enemies. With multiple Dragonkin, this triggers frequently and devastates grouped enemies.
Krowns + Food
3
Colonize aggressively
Dragon's civilians can enter neutral territory without penalties. Expand aggressively and use Thralls to work tiles you colonize.
Food per tile
4
Feast in November
Accelerate your Fame gain. At 200 Fame you receive 5 free Thralls and an extended Dragon Essence gauge — both are significant bonuses.
Food varies
5
Build Forge & upgrade tools
Upgrade Thrall production buildings and food sources. Thrall upgrades are cheap and compound well with their numbers.
60 Wood Iron + Krowns
🐉

Lore priority for 801:

1st Weaponsmith — +20% attack for all military units
2nd Feeling Safe — +3 Happiness with warchief, +1 per upgraded military camp
3rd Recruitment — +25% permanent population growth speed
4th Fur Coats — Your units no longer have reduced power during winter
Year 802

Draconic Nightmares & Full Push

Goal: Reach 500 Fame to unlock Draconic Nightmares — the upgraded form of Dragonkin. Push the enemy with your full Dragonkin army and Surtr leading the charge.

1
Convert 7–10 Dragonkin
Your primary military force. Dragonkin's Whirlwind ability hits all surrounding enemies — the more Dragonkin you have, the more frequently it triggers.
Krowns + Food
2
Reach 500 Fame: Draconic Nightmares
At 500 Fame, the Dragon Essence gauge is maximized and you unlock Draconic Nightmares — send Dragonkin through the Altar a second time to upgrade them. They gain increased health and defense.
500 Fame
3
Push the enemy
Draconic Nightmares with Surtr leading are one of the strongest military compositions in the game. Push into enemy territory and use the Sacrificial Pyre to maintain Dragon Essence during extended campaigns.
4
Keep buying Thralls
Thralls are your economic backbone. Keep buying them from your Dock and assigning them to production buildings. A well-managed Thrall economy can sustain a massive Dragonkin army.
Krowns per Thrall
⚔

Sacrifice timing: Use the Sacrificial Pyre strategically — sacrifice wounded or low-value units to spike your Dragon Essence and gain combat bonuses. Don't sacrifice your best Dragonkin unless you're in a desperate situation.

Year 803

Sustained Army & Map Control

Goal: Sustain 10+ Draconic Nightmares, keep your Thrall economy running, and use Surtr to lead decisive engagements.

1
Sustain 10+ Draconic Nightmares
Keep Dragonkin Altars upgraded and cycle Krown income into maintaining your warband. Draconic Nightmares are among the strongest individual units in the game.
2
Maintain Thrall economy
Keep buying Thralls and assigning them to production. A large Thrall workforce frees up your villagers for military conversion and keeps your Krown income stable.
3
Coordinate with team to close out
Dragon is a strong late-game carry. If your team has early-game clans (Wolf, Hound, Lynx), they should have created space for you to scale. Now it's time to deliver.
⚠

Watch your Krowns: Dragon's upkeep is the highest in the game. If your Krown income drops, your Dragonkin will start deserting. Always keep a Krown buffer and prioritize Dock/Trading Post upgrades.

Year 804

Endgame Domination

Goal: Close out the game with your Draconic Nightmare army. Dragon is one of the strongest late-game carries — if you've scaled properly, you should be unstoppable.

1
Military surge with 14+ Draconic Nightmares
A full warband of Draconic Nightmares with Surtr is one of the most powerful military compositions in Northgard. Push for Domination victory.
2
Use Sacrificial Pyre for combat spikes
In decisive battles, sacrifice units to maximize Dragon Essence and gain combat bonuses. Going into a deficit is fine in 804 — you won't need the resources after the game ends.
