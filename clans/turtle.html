Year 800

Foundation & Garrison Defense

Goal: Establish food and wood income, build your core production buildings, and start leveraging Turtle's unique garrison mechanic. When zones are attacked, civilian units automatically garrison in buildings and gain a Defense bonus when they exit.

1
Build Scout Camp
Scout all adjacent tiles. Turtle's garrison mechanic means you want dense, well-built zones — find good tiles for your core production buildings.
50 Wood
2
Expand Out
Prioritize food tiles and lore stones. Turtle's economy is flexible — adapt to your map. The garrison mechanic means your civilians are safer than other clans when attacked.
20 Food
3
Build core production buildings
Build food production, Woodcutter's Lodge, and a Trading Post. Turtle's economy scales with the number of buildings in each zone — build densely.
150 Wood
🐢

Garrison mechanic: When a zone is attacked, civilian units automatically garrison in buildings. When they exit, they gain a Defense bonus for a few seconds. This makes Turtle's civilians significantly more resilient than other clans — don't be afraid to build densely in contested zones.

4
Build Woodcutter's Lodge
Assign two villagers. Wood is needed for more buildings throughout 801. Turtle buys Villagers with Food — the cost increases with population size but decreases over time based on Happiness.
50 Wood
5
Build Docks or Trading Post
Steady Krown income funds Njörd and building upgrades. Turtle's economy is flexible — adapt to your map.
80 Wood
6
Summon Njörd
Njörd is your warchief — a powerful frontline fighter. Get him out as soon as you have the resources. His presence boosts your military significantly.
Krowns + Iron
🐢

Villager purchasing: Turtle buys Villagers with Food instead of waiting for natural population growth. The cost decreases over time based on Happiness — keep your Happiness high to reduce Villager costs and grow your population faster.

Year 801

Economy & Reach 200 Fame

Goal: Hit 200 Fame, keep buying Villagers with Food, and build out your military presence. Turtle's garrison mechanic makes your economy more resilient to raids than other clans.

1
Buy Villagers with Food
Keep buying Villagers to grow your population. The cost decreases over time based on Happiness — keep your Happiness high to reduce costs. Assign new Villagers to production buildings immediately.
Food per Villager
2
Build Forge & upgrade tools
Upgrade food production and Sailors. Turtle's economy scales well with tool upgrades and the garrison Defense bonus.
60 Wood Iron + Krowns
3
Colonize aggressively
Expand toward valuable tiles. Turtle's garrison mechanic means your civilians are safer in contested zones — don't be afraid to colonize near enemy borders.
Food per tile
4
Build Training Camp & recruit military
Build a Training Camp and start converting villagers. Turtle can field any standard unit — choose based on your team composition.
50 Wood
5
Feast in November
Accelerate your Fame gain. Keep your Happiness high to reduce Villager costs and grow your population faster.
Food varies
🐢

Lore priority for 801:

1st Weaponsmith — +20% attack for all military units
2nd Feeling Safe — +3 Happiness with warchief, +1 per upgraded military camp
3rd Recruitment — +25% permanent population growth speed
4th Carpentry Mastery — –20% building upgrade costs and upkeep
Year 802

Military Build-Up & Full Economy

Goal: Build out your military force, maximize your economy, and push toward 500 Fame. Turtle's garrison mechanic and Villager purchasing make them a resilient mid-game clan.

1
Convert 7+ military units
Turtle can field any standard unit. Choose based on your team composition. Warriors for frontline, Axe Throwers for ranged, Shield Bearers for defense.
Krowns + Food
2
Upgrade military buildings
More warband slots means more units in a single engagement.
Stone + Wood
3
Build Altar of Kings
Unlocks additional lore paths. Turtle's lore tree has powerful economic and military bonuses — prioritize the paths that complement your team composition.
Wood + Stone
4
Push the enemy
With a strong economy and military, Turtle is ready to push. Use Njörd to lead your army and target enemy production zones.
Year 803

Sustained Army & Map Control

Goal: Sustain 10+ military units, keep buying Villagers, and use Turtle's garrison mechanic to defend your economy while pushing the enemy.

1
Sustain 10+ military units in warband
Keep military camps upgraded and cycle Krown income into maintaining your warband. Turtle's Villager purchasing means you can replace losses faster than other clans.
2
Keep buying Villagers
Keep your Happiness high to reduce Villager costs. A large population means more workers on production buildings and more military units.
3
Coordinate with team to close out
Turtle is a strong support-economy clan. If your team has military carries, keep your economy running and let them do the fighting.
Year 804

Endgame Support

Goal: Close out with your team. Turtle's garrison mechanic and Villager purchasing make them a resilient endgame support clan.

1
Keep your economy running
Turtle's garrison mechanic means your economy is more resilient to raids than other clans. Keep buying Villagers and assigning them to production buildings.
2
Support your team's final push
Use Njörd and your military to support your team's final push. Turtle's garrison Defense bonus makes your zones harder to take — use this to hold key positions.
