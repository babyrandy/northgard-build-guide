Year 800

Foundation & Eitria & Brok

Goal: Get Eitria and Brok working immediately — they replace Miners, Smiths, and Builders and work 20% faster. Start mining and forging as early as possible.

1
Build Scout Camp
Scout all adjacent tiles. Look for Iron and Stone deposits — Eitria and Brok need ore to forge. You receive 50% less Krowns from Ruins but can discover 5 Stone from Ruins and 5 Iron from Shipwrecks.
50 Wood
2
Expand Out
Prioritize tiles with Iron and Stone deposits. Eitria and Brok's mining speed is 20% faster than standard Miners — maximize their output by finding good ore tiles early.
20 Food
3
Build Völund's Forge
Replaces the standard Forge. You can summon Eitria and Brok here without Iron — a significant cost reduction. Build it immediately and assign both warchiefs.
60 Wood
🐴

Eitria and Brok: They replace Miners, Smiths, and Builders. They build, repair, mine, and forge 20% faster than their counterparts. When in the same zone, they mine and forge 50% faster (200 Fame bonus). Keep them together whenever possible.

4
Build Woodcutter's Lodge + Food Production
Assign two villagers to wood and build a food source. Horse needs a stable economy to sustain Eitria and Brok's forging operations.
100 Wood
5
Build Iron Mine
Eitria and Brok need Iron to forge. Build an Iron Mine immediately and assign them to it. Their 20% faster mining speed means you'll have Iron significantly sooner than other clans.
60 Wood 10 Krowns
6
Build Docks or Trading Post
Steady Krown income funds building upgrades. At 200 Fame, selling Stone or Iron earns 20% more Krowns — build your ore economy early to capitalize on this.
80 Wood
🐴

Lore priority for 800:

1st Sharp Axes — +20% Wood production
2nd Colonization — –30% food cost to colonize new territories
3rd City Builder — improving a zone is 50% cheaper; specialized units get +10% production and –50% upkeep
4th Weaponsmith — +20% attack for all military units
Year 801

Economy & Reach 200 Fame

Goal: Hit 200 Fame to unlock Eitria and Brok's 50% faster mining/forging when together, and the 20% Krown bonus from selling Stone and Iron. This is your economic power spike.

1
Keep Eitria and Brok together
After 200 Fame, Eitria and Brok mine and forge 50% faster when in the same zone. Always keep them together — their combined output is significantly higher than working separately.
2
Sell Stone and Iron for Krowns
At 200 Fame, selling Stone or Iron earns 20% more Krowns. Use your surplus ore to generate Krown income. Neutral factions like the Jötnar will also accept Stone and Iron in trade.
+20% Krowns from ore sales
3
Build Military Camps
Build one of each military camp type. Horse's Leadership lore path benefits from having multiple military building types — each type adds to your military power.
150 Wood
4
Upgrade tools aggressively
Eitria and Brok's faster forging means you can upgrade tools significantly faster than other clans. Prioritize Merchant/Sailor upgrades first, then food production, then military.
Iron + Krowns
5
Colonize aggressively
Look for more Iron and Stone deposits. At 500 Fame, 2 additional Stone and 2 Iron deposits appear in your territory — plan your expansion to maximize ore access.
Food per tile
Year 802

Military Build-Up & Dainsleif & Tyrfing

Goal: Forge the Dainsleif & Tyrfing relic, build out your military force, and push toward 500 Fame for 2 additional ore deposits and the 15% production bonus near relics.

1
Forge Dainsleif & Tyrfing relic
Eitria and Brok gain +20% attack, defense, and health, plus 20% faster mining and forging. This makes them even more powerful in both combat and production.
Iron + Krowns
2
Convert 7+ military units
Horse can field any standard unit. Choose based on your team composition. Warriors for frontline, Axe Throwers for ranged, Shield Bearers for defense.
Krowns + Food
3
Upgrade military buildings
More warband slots means more units in a single engagement. Eitria and Brok's faster building speed means upgrades complete significantly faster than for other clans.
Stone + Wood
4
Reach 500 Fame: Legacy of the Earth
At 500 Fame, 2 additional Stone and 2 Iron deposits appear in your territory. Zones with or adjacent to an active relic receive a 15% production bonus. This is a massive economic and military boost.
500 Fame
⚔

Relic placement: At 500 Fame, zones adjacent to your active relic get a 15% production bonus. Place your relic in a central zone to maximize the number of production buildings that benefit from this bonus.

Year 803

Sustained Army & Map Control

Goal: Sustain 10+ military units, keep Eitria and Brok forging upgrades, and use the 15% relic production bonus to maintain your economic advantage.

1
Sustain 10+ military units in warband
Keep military camps upgraded and cycle Krown income into maintaining your warband. Horse's faster forging means you can upgrade units significantly faster than other clans.
2
Keep Eitria and Brok forging
Never let Eitria and Brok sit idle. They should always be mining, forging, or building. Their 50% faster combined output is your most valuable economic asset.
3
Coordinate with team to close out
Horse is a strong late-game carry. If your team has early-game clans, they should have created space for you to scale. Now it's time to deliver.
Year 804

Endgame Domination

Goal: Close out with Domination, Fame, or Lore Victory. Horse's versatile economy and Eitria and Brok's forging make all victory conditions achievable.

1
Military surge with fully upgraded units
Horse's faster forging means your units are more upgraded than the enemy's. A fully upgraded army with Dainsleif & Tyrfing buffs is extremely powerful.
2
Use Eitria and Brok in combat
With Dainsleif & Tyrfing, Eitria and Brok are powerful combat units. Use them to lead your army — their +20% attack, defense, and health make them formidable fighters.
