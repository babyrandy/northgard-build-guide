Year 800

Foundation & Harbor Economy

Goal: Build your Harbor, start scouting aggressively for Lore and Fame, and establish your Krown economy. Raven colonizes with Krowns instead of food — your economy is your military.

1
Build Scout Camp
Scout all adjacent tiles immediately. Raven gains Lore when scouting new zones and Fame when discovering Neutral Factions and enemy Town Halls — every new zone is free resources.
50 Wood
2
Build Harbor
Replaces the Longship Dock. Lets you spend Krowns to explore a random zone or send mercenaries to attack enemies. This is your core economic and military building — build it immediately.
80 Wood
3
Expand Out with Krowns
Raven colonizes with Krowns instead of food. This frees up your food for population growth. Expand aggressively — every new zone gives Lore and Fame.
Krowns per tile
🐦

Krown colonization: Raven uses Krowns instead of food to colonize. This means your food goes entirely to population growth. Build your Krown economy first — Trading Posts and Harbors are your priority.

4
Build Woodcutter's Lodge + Food Production
Assign two villagers to wood and build a food source. You still need food for population growth even though you colonize with Krowns.
100 Wood
5
Build Trading Post
Additional Krown income. Raven's economy is Krown-dependent — the more Krowns you generate, the more mercenaries you can send and the faster you can colonize.
60 Wood
6
Summon Liv
Liv is your warchief. She can use Odin's Eyes to see through the Fog of War for one month — invaluable for scouting enemy positions before sending mercenaries.
Krowns + Iron
🐦

Scouting is income: Every new zone you scout gives Lore. Every Neutral Faction and enemy Town Hall you discover gives Fame. Keep your scouts active at all times — Raven's passive income from exploration is significant.

Year 801

Economy & Reach 200 Fame

Goal: Hit 200 Fame to unlock mercenary raids on enemy beaches. Build out your Krown economy and start sending mercenaries to harass enemies.

1
Build more Harbors
More Harbors means more mercenary capacity and more Krown income. Build 2–3 Harbors if you have coastal tiles.
80 Wood
2
Continue scouting aggressively
Keep your scouts active. Every new zone is free Lore. Use Liv's Odin's Eyes to scout enemy positions before sending mercenaries.
3
Send mercenaries to harass enemies
Use your Harbor to send mercenaries to attack enemy beaches. This forces enemies to spend resources defending instead of expanding. Target their production buildings.
Krowns per raid
4
Feast in November
Accelerate your Fame gain. At 200 Fame, you can raid enemy beaches without Town Halls — a significant expansion of your mercenary options.
Food varies
5
Build Forge & upgrade Sailors
Upgraded Sailors generate more Krowns from your Harbors. This is your most important upgrade — more Krowns means more mercenaries and faster colonization.
60 Wood Iron + Krowns
🐦

Lore priority for 801:

1st Weaponsmith — +20% attack for all military units
2nd Feeling Safe — +3 Happiness with warchief, +1 per upgraded military camp
3rd Negotiation — –50% Marketplace prices, 20% faster stock replenishment
4th Recruitment — +25% permanent population growth speed
Year 802

Mercenary Raids & Naglfar Relic

Goal: Forge the Naglfar relic, scale your mercenary raids, and push toward 500 Fame to unlock reduced building upkeep and direct mercenary recruitment from military buildings.

1
Forge Naglfar relic
Grants your ships invulnerability, allowing them to traverse even the most dangerous waters. Also lets you recruit a Son of Hrymr to join your mercenary raids — significantly boosting their offensive strength.
Iron + Krowns
2
Scale mercenary raids
With Naglfar, your raids become significantly more powerful. Target enemy production buildings and Town Halls. Be strategic — attack one clan at a time to avoid disrupting your trade flow.
Krowns per raid
3
Build Training Camp for defense
Raven's mercenaries handle offense, but you need a military presence for defense. Build a Training Camp and keep 5–7 units in your warband.
50 Wood
4
Reach 500 Fame
At 500 Fame, building upkeep costs are reduced, you can recruit mercenaries directly from military buildings, and your Raiders can attack enemy Town Halls. This is a massive power spike.
500 Fame
⚔

Raid timing: Be strategic when raiding. Attack one clan at a time rather than multiple clans simultaneously. Systematic pressure is more effective than scattered raids, and it won't disrupt your trade flow.

Year 803

Sustained Raids & Trade Victory

Goal: Keep raiding, maintain your Krown economy, and push toward a Trade Victory. Raven is one of the best Trade Victory clans in the game.

1
Maintain Krown economy
Keep your Harbors and Trading Posts running. Upgraded Sailors are your most important economic investment — they generate significantly more Krowns.
2
Keep raiding systematically
Use Liv's Odin's Eyes to scout enemy positions before each raid. Target their most valuable production buildings — Forges, Docks, and food sources.
3
Build Lighthouse for Trade Victory
If going for a Trade Victory, build a Lighthouse and accumulate 2000 Commercial Influence. Raven's Krown economy and Harbor network make this the most achievable victory condition.
Year 804

Endgame & Victory

Goal: Close out with a Trade Victory or Domination. Raven's mercenary raids and Krown economy make both viable.

1
Push for Trade Victory
Accumulate 2000 Commercial Influence via your Lighthouse. Raven's Harbor network and Krown economy make this the fastest path to victory.
2
Or push for Domination with mercenaries
If Trade Victory isn't viable, use your mercenary army and Naglfar to push for Domination. Son of Hrymr + mercenaries is a powerful late-game military composition.
