Year 800

Foundation & First Lore

Goal: Establish stable Food and Wood income, begin lore accumulation, and get Forging your relic before winter.

1
Build Scout Camp
First build of the game. Scout all adjacent tiles immediately — you're looking for Lore Stones, forest tiles, and food sources.
50 Wood
2
Expand to Lore Stones or Forest Tiles
Prioritize colonizing tiles with Lore Stones for early lore income. Food tiles are your next priority. Tiles give +2 max population each. Don't expand blindly; wait until scouting reveals a worthwhile tile.
20–40 Food
3
Take Simple Living Lore
Use your first earned lore to unlock Simple Living — it reduces food consumption, which is critical for sustaining expansion in the early game without starving your population.
Lore
4
Build Woodcutter's Lodge & Iron Mine
Get wood income running as soon as you have Simple Living. Assign two villagers. You don't need as much wood as others . Don't delay this past your second expansion.
50 Wood
4
First Lore Building
Build a Mender's hut. Assign a villager to it immediately to start generating lore.
60 Wood +Lore/month
6
Build Forge
Once you have enough krowns and Iron, build your clan relic: Mask of Gullinbursti.
Food Lore
⚠

Don't neglect food: The Boar Clan's lore focus can tempt you to over-invest in knowledge buildings early. Keep at least one food source assigned at all times — running out of Food in winter will stall your entire build.

Year 801

Lore Engine & Peaceful Expansion

Goal: Expand Lore income, grow your population by colonizing wildlife tiles, and establish a Krowns economy through a Trading Post before winter.

1
Take Colonization Lore
Unlock Colonization from the lore tree. This reduces the food cost of colonizing new territories, which is essential for the Boar Clan's expansion strategy — especially when targeting wildlife tiles that carry an extra food surcharge.
Lore
3
Take Lay of the Land Lore
Lay of the Land increases the production bonus from Forests, Swaps, and Fish Tiles.
Lore
4
Expand into Wildlife Tiles
Wolves and brown bears in your colonized zones become friendly. Colonize adjacent wildlife tiles — each new territory grants +2 max population. The extra food cost is worth it: you gain population cap without needing to fight the animals first.
Food + extra (wildlife)
5
Build Trading Post or Docks
Establish a Krowns income source. Build a Trading Post. Krowns funds future building upgrades and keeps your economy flexible heading into Year 802.
60–80 Wood
6
Feast in November
Hold a feast in November to accelerate Fame gain. Reaching 200 Fame unlocks the Boar Clan's wildlife healing mechanic.
Food varies
🐗

Wildlife tiles are free real estate: Each colonized territory gives +2 max population — and wildlife tiles are no exception. Stack as many as you can reach.

Lore Priority — Year 801

1st Simple Living — reduces Wood consumption, sustaining your growing population through expansion
2nd Colonization — lowers the food cost of claiming new territories, enabling faster peaceful expansion
3rd Lay of the Land — increases the production bonus per territory.
Year 802

Economy Scaling & Military Prep

Goal: Scale Lore output through Bartering and Handiwork upgrades, build a credible military presence with Axe Throwers and Shieldbearers, and leverage the Boar Clan's compounding knowledge bonuses before winter.

1
Take Bartering Lore
Bartering gives you more Krowns and Happiness. It also allows you to trade with neutrals and become allies.
Lore
2
Take Handiwork Lore
Handiwork reduces the upkeep for all your buildings. It also increases production by 15%.
Lore
3
Recruit Svarn
By this point begin building up your army. Svarn will allow you to recruit an animal you can control.
150 Krowns + 5 Iron
4
Build Altar of Kings
The Altar of Kings unlocks the Boar Clan's 500 Fame ability and opens additional lore paths unavailable through standard buildings. Build it once your wood and stone reserves are stable — Handiwork lore reduces the cost. Assign a Shaman to the Altar to maximize its lore output and accelerate your path to the endgame knowledge bonuses.
Wood Stone + Krowns +Lore/month
🐗

Peaceful scaling vs. military prep: If your map position is secure and no enemy is threatening, stay on the lore path — Bartering and Handiwork compound into significant economic leads by Year 803. If you're seeing early aggression, shift 5–6 villagers to military and delay the Altar. Don't try to do both at full speed; pick a lane based on what the map demands.

⚠

Don't over-invest in stone: The Boar Clan's strength comes from Lore and population, not upgraded infrastructure. A second Stone Mine is rarely worth it in 802 — the villager is better assigned to a lore building or food source. Only build additional stone capacity if you have a specific upgrade queued that demands it.

Year 803

Lore Completion & Map Control

Goal: Sustain your Lore advantage, maintain a credible military presence, and control key map areas through territory expansion and Svarn's tamed animal.

1
Take Osmosis Lore
Osmosis is the Boar Clan's premier late-lore pick — it generates Happiness the more land you hold.
Lore
2
Sustain Military Presence
Keep 8-12 Axe throwers and Shieldbearers active and positioned near your borders. You don't need to be aggressive, but a visible military deters raids and buys time for your team to finish pulling ahead. Reassign villagers from food surplus to military if you're under pressure.
Food Krowns
🐗

Osmosis scales with territory: Every tile you control increases the Happiness Osmosis gives. Maximize your territory footprint before taking Osmosis — colonize any reachable tiles in early 803 first, then unlock the lore.

⚠

Don't abandon military entirely: The Boar Clan's lore lead can create a false sense of security. By Year 803, aggressive clans like Wolf or Bear may have scaled their armies significantly. Keep at least a token military force and watch your borders — losing territory now directly reduces your Osmosis lore income.

Year 804

Win Condition Execution

Goal: Execute the win condition using accumulated Lore and knowledge bonuses — push for Fame victory through territory control and feasts.

2
Fame Victory via Territory & Feasts
Hold as many territories as possible — each one contributes to your Fame total — and hold feasts in November and March to spike your Fame gain. The Boar Clan's wide territorial footprint from peaceful expansion makes this a reliable fallback win condition.
Food (feasts)
