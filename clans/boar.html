Year 800

Foundation & First Lore

Goal: Establish stable Food and Wood income, begin lore accumulation, and get Svarn on the map before winter.

1
Build Scout Camp
First build of the game. Scout all adjacent tiles immediately — you're looking for Lore Stones, forest tiles, and food sources. Assign a villager to the Town Hall to keep food flowing.
50 Wood
2
Expand to Lore Stones or Forest Tiles
Prioritize colonizing tiles with Lore Stones for early lore income. Forest tiles are your next best target — they feed your wood economy and give +2 max population each. Don't expand blindly; wait until scouting reveals a worthwhile tile.
20–40 Food
3
Build Woodcutter's Lodge
Get wood income running as soon as you have a forest tile. Assign two villagers. You'll need a steady wood supply for the buildings coming in 801. Don't delay this past your second expansion.
50 Wood
4
Build First Lore Building (Runestone or Study)
The Boar Clan's strength compounds with lore — get your first dedicated lore building up before winter. A Runestone on a Lore Stone tile is ideal. Assign a villager to it immediately to start generating lore each month.
50 Wood +Lore/month
5
Take Simple Living Lore
Your starting bonus gives you Free Knowledge +1. Use your first earned lore to unlock Simple Living — it reduces food consumption, which is critical for sustaining expansion in the early game without starving your population.
Lore
6
Summon Svarn
Once you have enough gold, summon Svarn. He heals friendly animals in his zone and can tame one wolf or bear — giving you early map control without combat. Place him near a wildlife tile you plan to colonize next.
Food Lore
🐗

Wildlife coexistence: Once you reach 200 Fame, wolves and brown bears in your colonized zones become friendly — they won't attack your villagers. This means you can safely colonize wildlife tiles for the extra Food cost and gain +2 max population per territory without clearing the animals first.

⚠

Don't neglect food: The Boar Clan's lore focus can tempt you to over-invest in knowledge buildings early. Keep at least one food source assigned at all times — running out of Food in winter will stall your entire build.

Year 801

Lore Engine & Peaceful Expansion

Goal: Expand Lore income, grow your population by colonizing wildlife tiles, and establish a gold economy through a Trading Post or Docks before winter.

1
Build Shamans
The Shaman is the Boar Clan's unique unit — recruit one or two from your lore building. Shamans generate lore passively and provide buffs to nearby villagers, making them the backbone of your knowledge engine. Assign them to your lore buildings to maximize monthly output.
+Lore/month
2
Take Colonization Lore
Unlock Colonization from the lore tree. This reduces the food cost of colonizing new territories, which is essential for the Boar Clan's expansion strategy — especially when targeting wildlife tiles that carry an extra food surcharge.
Lore
3
Take Lay of the Land Lore
Lay of the Land increases the population cap bonus you receive from each new territory. Combined with Colonization, this makes every tile you claim significantly more valuable — more land means more people, which means more workers and more lore.
Lore
4
Expand into Wildlife Tiles
Once you hit 200 Fame, wolves and brown bears in your colonized zones become friendly. Colonize adjacent wildlife tiles — each new territory grants +2 max population. The extra food cost is worth it: you gain population cap without needing to fight the animals first.
Food + extra (wildlife)
5
Build Trading Post or Docks
Establish a gold income source. If you have coastal access, a Longship Dock provides steady passive gold. Otherwise, build a Trading Post. Gold funds future building upgrades and keeps your economy flexible heading into Year 802.
60–80 Wood
6
Feast in November
Hold a feast in November to accelerate Fame gain. Reaching 200 Fame unlocks the Boar Clan's wildlife coexistence mechanic — a critical milestone that opens up peaceful colonization of wildlife tiles for the rest of the game.
Food varies
🐗

Wildlife tiles are free real estate: Each colonized territory gives +2 max population — and wildlife tiles are no exception. After hitting 200 Fame, wolves and bears in your zones become friendly, so you can colonize those tiles for just the extra Food cost without any combat. Stack as many as you can reach.

Lore Priority — Year 801

1st Simple Living — reduces food consumption, sustaining your growing population through expansion
2nd Colonization — lowers the food cost of claiming new territories, enabling faster peaceful expansion
3rd Lay of the Land — increases the population cap bonus per territory, compounding the value of every tile you colonize
Year 802

Economy Scaling & Military Prep

Goal: Scale Lore output through Bartering and Handiwork upgrades, build a credible military presence with Swordsmen or Warriors, and leverage the Boar Clan's compounding knowledge bonuses before winter.

1
Take Bartering Lore
Bartering reduces the gold cost of trading actions and improves your exchange rates. For the Boar Clan, whose lore tree unlocks compound economic bonuses, this frees up gold to invest in buildings and military without stalling your knowledge engine. Take it early in 802 before your gold reserves are committed elsewhere.
Lore
2
Take Handiwork Lore
Handiwork reduces the wood and stone cost of building construction and upgrades. This is a high-value lore pick for the Boar Clan — you'll be building the Forge, upgrading lore buildings, and potentially constructing the Altar of Kings this year. Every resource saved here compounds into more lore and military capacity.
Lore
3
Build Forge & Upgrade Tools
Build the Forge and upgrade tools for your Woodcutters, Farmers, and Shamans. Tool upgrades directly amplify the Boar Clan's lore-based production bonuses — a Shaman with upgraded tools generates noticeably more lore per month. With Handiwork active, the Forge itself costs less to build.
80 Wood Iron + Gold
4
Recruit Swordsmen or Warriors
The Boar Clan is not a military clan, but you need a credible defense by mid-802. Recruit 3–5 Swordsmen or Warriors from your Town Hall. They won't win aggressive engagements, but they deter raids and protect your lore buildings long enough for your knowledge bonuses to pull ahead. Assign Svarn nearby to boost their morale.
Food Gold
5
Build Altar of Kings
The Altar of Kings unlocks the Boar Clan's 500 Fame ability and opens additional lore paths unavailable through standard buildings. Build it once your wood and stone reserves are stable — Handiwork lore reduces the cost. Assign a Shaman to the Altar to maximize its lore output and accelerate your path to the endgame knowledge bonuses.
Wood Stone + Gold +Lore/month
🐗

Peaceful scaling vs. military prep: If your map position is secure and no enemy is threatening, stay on the lore path — Bartering and Handiwork compound into significant economic leads by Year 803. If you're seeing early aggression, shift 2–3 villagers to military and delay the Altar. Don't try to do both at full speed; pick a lane based on what the map demands.

⚠

Don't over-invest in stone: The Boar Clan's strength comes from Lore and population, not stone-heavy infrastructure. A second Stone Mine is rarely worth it in 802 — the villager is better assigned to a lore building or food source. Only build additional stone capacity if you have a specific upgrade queued that demands it.

Year 803

Lore Completion & Map Control

Goal: Sustain your Lore advantage, maintain a credible military presence, and control key map areas through territory expansion and Svarn's tamed animal.

1
Take Osmosis Lore
Osmosis is the Boar Clan's premier late-lore pick — it generates passive lore based on the number of territories you control and the number of neighboring clans. The more land you hold, the more lore you earn each month without assigning a single worker. Take it as soon as it's available in 803.
Lore
2
Complete the Lore Tree
With Osmosis active and your Shamans still generating lore, push to finish the remaining lore tree upgrades. Prioritize anything that boosts production or population. Completing the tree gives you a compounding economic lead that most clans cannot match in the late game.
Lore
3
Upgrade Town Hall
Upgrade your Town Hall to increase population growth speed and unlock higher-tier villager assignments. A larger population means more Shamans, more workers, and more military capacity — all of which compound your lore and economic output heading into Year 804.
Wood Gold
4
Sustain Military Presence
Keep 4–6 Swordsmen or Warriors active and positioned near your borders. You don't need to be aggressive, but a visible military deters raids and buys time for your lore engine to finish pulling ahead. Reassign villagers from food surplus to military if you're under pressure.
Food Gold
5
Deploy Svarn's Tamed Animal for Scouting & Harassment
If Svarn has a tamed bear or wolf, move it into neutral or enemy-adjacent territory. The tamed animal provides vision of enemy movements and can harass undefended workers or delay expansion. It's a low-cost way to project map presence without committing your military.
Svarn ability
🐗

Osmosis scales with territory: Every tile you control increases the passive lore Osmosis generates each month. Maximize your territory footprint before taking Osmosis — colonize any reachable tiles in early 803 first, then unlock the lore. More neighbors also means more lore, so a crowded map is actually an advantage for the Boar Clan.

⚠

Don't abandon military entirely: The Boar Clan's lore lead can create a false sense of security. By Year 803, aggressive clans like Wolf or Bear may have scaled their armies significantly. Keep at least a token military force and watch your borders — losing territory now directly reduces your Osmosis lore income.

Year 804

Win Condition Execution

Goal: Execute the win condition using accumulated Lore and knowledge bonuses — push for Fame victory through territory control, feasts, and Svarn's 500 Fame endgame ability.

1
Activate Altar of Kings — Wisdom Victory Path
With the Altar of Kings built and your lore tree complete, activate the Wisdom victory path. The Boar Clan's compounding knowledge bonuses make this the most natural win condition — every lore unlock you've made since Year 800 has been building toward this moment. Assign Shamans to the Altar to maximize its output in the final push.
Lore Gold
2
Fame Victory via Territory & Feasts
If the Wisdom path is contested or too slow, pivot to a Fame victory. Hold as many territories as possible — each one contributes to your Fame total — and hold feasts in November and March to spike your Fame gain. The Boar Clan's wide territorial footprint from peaceful expansion makes this a reliable fallback win condition.
Food (feasts)
3
Trigger Svarn's 500 Fame Endgame Ability
Once you reach 500 Fame, Svarn's endgame ability unlocks through the Altar of Kings. Use it to deliver a decisive final push — whether that's a military surge, a territorial claim, or a knowledge-based victory spike. Time it for maximum impact: coordinate with a feast or a territorial expansion to compound the Fame gain in a single turn.
500 Fame
🐗

The Boar Clan's win condition is knowledge accumulation: Every new knowledge unlock on the lore tree gives +5 Fame. This means every lore investment you've made since Year 800 has been quietly building your Fame total. By Year 804, a fully completed lore tree translates directly into a commanding Fame lead — making the Boar Clan one of the strongest late-game Fame victory clans in the right hands.
