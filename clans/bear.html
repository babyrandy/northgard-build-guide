Year 800

Foundation & Kaija the Armored Bear

Goal: Get Kaija on the map, establish food and wood income, and start building toward Borgild. Bear's winter resilience means you can play aggressively even in the cold months.

1
Build Scout Camp
Your very first build. Scout all adjacent tiles — you're looking for food sources, lore stones, and fish tiles (Kaija regenerates health near fish).
50 Wood
2
Expand Out
Prioritize food tiles and lore stones. Kaija starts on the map with you — she can help clear neutrals immediately.
20 Food
3
Build House + Training Camp
Build a House for population growth and a Training Camp to recruit Shield Maidens — your primary military unit. Shield Maidens start with improved weapons thanks to Bear's starting bonus.
100 Wood
🐻

Kaija the Armored Bear: Kaija starts on the map with you. She regenerates health near fish tiles and produces food passively. Keep her near fish when not fighting. She's a powerful early-game unit — use her to clear neutrals alongside your Shield Maidens.

4
Build Woodcutter's Lodge
Assign two villagers. Wood is needed for more buildings throughout 801. Bear is wood-hungry due to building upgrades.
50 Wood
5
First Lore: Hearthstone
Reduces extra Firewood consumption during winter by 50% and reduces the winter food penalty by 20%. Bear's winter resilience is your core identity — lean into it early.
Lore
6
Build Docks or Trading Post
You need Krowns to summon Borgild. Start your economy early. Docks are preferred if you have coastal tiles.
80 Wood
7
Summon Borgild
Borgild is your warchief — a powerful Shield Maiden with high defense. She synergizes with the Scabbard of Gram relic, which freezes enemies on hit. Get her out as soon as you have the resources.
Krowns + Iron
🐻

Winter is your friend: Bear's units are 10% more resistant during winter and suffer no food/firewood penalties with Hearthstone. While other clans slow down in winter, you can keep pushing. Time your aggressive moves for the cold months.

⚠

Build houses: You want population headroom to convert Shield Maidens in 801–802. Don't neglect housing.

Year 801

Economy & Reach 200 Fame

Goal: Hit 200 Fame to unlock Kaija and Borgild's production bonus. Build out your economy and start converting Shield Maidens.

1
Build Longship Dock(s) or Trading Post
Steady Krown income funds Shield Maiden recruitment and building upgrades. If you have 2+ coastal tiles, build a second dock.
80 Wood
2
Continue Lore & Build Forge
Build a Forge and start upgrading your Shield Maidens and food production. The Scabbard of Gram relic gives Shield Maidens +10% attack and defense.
60 Wood
3
Colonize aggressively & Clear
Expand toward wolf dens and fish tiles. Use Kaija and Borgild to clear harder camps. Keep Kaija near fish tiles when not fighting so she regenerates health.
Food per tile
4
Feast in November
Accelerate your Fame gain. After 200 Fame, Kaija and Borgild boost production in their zone by 15% — a significant economic bonus.
Food varies
5
Forge Scabbard of Gram
Your clan relic. Borgild wields the sword Gram — enemies hit are frozen along with the surrounding area. Also gives +10% attack and defense to Shield Maidens. This is a game-changing combat tool.
Iron + Krowns
🐻

Lore priority for 801:

1st Hearthstone — –50% winter firewood consumption, –20% winter food penalty
2nd Weaponsmith — +20% attack for all military units
3rd Feeling Safe — +3 Happiness with warchief, +1 per upgraded military camp
4th Carpentry Mastery — –20% building upgrade costs and upkeep
Year 802

Full Shield Maiden Convert & Military Build-Up

Goal: Mass convert Shield Maidens, upgrade your Training Camp, and prepare to contest or rush enemy positions. Borgild's freeze ability makes your army devastating in coordinated fights.

1
Convert 7+ Shield Maidens
Shield Maidens start with improved weapons (Bear's starting bonus). Combined with Scabbard of Gram's +10% buff, they're significantly stronger than standard Shield Bearers.
Krowns + Food
2
Upgrade Training Camp
More warband slots means more Shield Maidens in a single engagement. Carpentry Mastery lore reduces upgrade costs by 20%.
Stone + Wood
3
Upgrade food buildings and Woodcutters
Bear scales well with upgraded infrastructure. Upgrade 1–2 food buildings and your Woodcutter's Lodge to sustain your growing warband.
Stone + Wood
4
Push in winter
Bear's units are 10% more resistant in winter and suffer no power reduction. Time your major pushes for winter — your enemies will be weakened while you're at full strength.
⚔

Borgild's freeze combo: Use Borgild's Scabbard of Gram to freeze enemy units, then pile in with Shield Maidens. The freeze effect hits the surrounding area — a well-timed use can lock down an entire enemy warband for several seconds.

Year 803

Sustained Army & Map Control

Goal: Sustain 10+ Shield Maidens, keep Kaija near fish tiles for passive food, and use Borgild's freeze to win decisive engagements.

1
Sustain 10+ Shield Maidens in warband
Keep military camps upgraded and cycle Krown income into maintaining your warband. Shield Maidens with Scabbard buffs are among the strongest defensive units in the game.
2
Reach 500 Fame: The Bear Awakens
At 500 Fame, you gain +3 Fame per enemy unit killed in zones with Borgild, military units gain 1.5% power per 100 Fame, and Kaija can enter enemy territory. This is a massive power spike.
3
Deploy Kaija in enemy territory
After 500 Fame, Kaija can enter enemy zones. She's a powerful tank that draws fire and regenerates health near fish. Use her to harass enemy production zones.
⚠

Watch for ranged units: By 803–804, enemy axe throwers and Trackers can kite your Shield Maidens. Use Borgild's freeze to close the gap and force melee engagements where your Shield Maidens excel.

Year 804

500 Fame & Endgame

Goal: Leverage The Bear Awakens bonus and Kaija's expanded range to close out the game. Bear is a strong late-game clan when properly scaled.

1
Military surge with 14+ Shield Maidens
With 500 Fame, your military units gain 1.5% power per 100 Fame. At 500 Fame that's +7.5% — and it keeps scaling. A fully scaled Bear army is extremely difficult to stop.
2
Use Borgild's freeze for decisive battles
Save the Scabbard of Gram freeze for your most important engagements. A well-timed freeze on the enemy's full warband can win the game outright.
3
Push for Domination or Fame victory
Bear wins most naturally via Domination (destroy enemy Town Halls) or Fame. Keep Borgild in zones with enemies to generate +3 Fame per kill.
